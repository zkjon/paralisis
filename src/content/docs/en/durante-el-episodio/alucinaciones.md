---
title: hallucinations
description: how hallucinations manifest during sleep paralysis
---

hallucinations in sleep paralysis are perceptions that feel real while awareness is already active.
they can be visual, auditory, or tactile, and they often appear together with bodily immobility.

---

## why they appear

during the episode, rem-related elements can remain active while the person is already perceiving the real environment.
this overlap makes it easier for the brain to complete ambiguous signals with threatening or unusual content.
it is not voluntary "imagining," but an automatic perceptual construction.

---

## most frequent forms

although the experience varies, there are recurring patterns:

- sensed presence in the room
- figures or shadows near the bed
- sounds such as footsteps, voices, or buzzing
- perceived physical contact, such as pressure or touch

these perceptions can appear alone or combined within the same episode.

---

## the role of fear

emotional state modulates the content of hallucinations.
when fear is intense, the brain tends to interpret uncertain stimuli as threats.
that is why, in many cases, the experience takes on a hostile tone even without clear external stimuli.

---

## types of hallucinatory content

in practical terms, they are usually grouped into three categories:

- intruder presence: perception that "someone" is nearby
- chest pressure: sensation of a load or entity on the chest
- vestibular-motor experiences: sensation of floating, falling, or separating from the body

these categories can overlap and change from one episode to another.

---

## perceptual realism

a central feature is their realism.
the person usually perceives hallucinations as external events rather than internal thoughts.
this explains why the memory of the episode can remain vivid for a long time.

---

## understanding and context

understanding that these hallucinations arise from a transitory neurophysiological state allows them to be reinterpreted more accurately.
although they feel real at the moment, they are part of the same temporary mismatch between rem sleep and wakefulness that defines sleep paralysis.
