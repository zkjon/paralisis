---
title: what is sleep paralysis?
description: a clear explanation of the state in which the mind wakes up before the body
---

sleep paralysis is a transitional state in which a person is conscious but unable to move or speak.

it is not a dream itself, but a disruption in the transition between sleep and wakefulness.

---

## a transitional state

to understand this phenomenon, it is important to see it as what it is: an intermediate point.

the body does not switch instantly from “asleep” to “awake”, but moves through transitional phases.

sleep paralysis occurs when that transition does not complete in a synchronized way.

---

## an active mind, an inactive body

in this state, the person regains awareness of their surroundings:

- they know where they are  
- they can perceive what is happening around them  

but the body remains temporarily inactive.

this combination — an active mind and an immobile body — defines the phenomenon.

---

## not a failure, but a mismatch

sleep paralysis is not a failure of the body, but a momentary desynchronization.

the processes that regulate sleep are still functioning, but not at the same pace.

as a result, the experience may feel strange or unsettling, but it is part of how the brain operates during sleep.

---

## a recognizable phenomenon

although it can be experienced in different ways, all cases of sleep paralysis share a common structure:

- it occurs during the transition between sleep and wakefulness  
- it involves physical immobility  
- there is awareness of the surroundings  

from this point on, the differences between experiences become more complex and are explored in other sections.
